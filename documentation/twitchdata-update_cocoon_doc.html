📝 Table Summary:

💡 The table is mainly about: Twitch Channel

Details: The table provides a comprehensive overview of various metrics related to Twitch channels. At its core, it captures the streaming activity and audience engagement of different broadcasters on the platform. The Channel attribute identifies the unique name of each Twitch channel. The Watch time(Minutes) attribute indicates the total amount of time viewers have spent watching a channel, while Stream time(minutes) reflects the total duration the channel has been live and streaming content. The Peak viewers attribute shows the highest number of concurrent viewers a channel has had at any point, and Average viewers represents the average number of viewers watching the channel during streams. The Followers attribute provides the total number of users who have followed the channel, suggesting the channel's popularity and reach. This is complemented by Followers gained, which tracks the increase in followers over a certain period. Similarly, Views gained measures the total number of video views accumulated by the channel, offering insight into overall content viewership. The Partnered attribute indicates whether a channel has entered into a partnership with Twitch, which typically means access to additional features and revenue options. The Mature attribute denotes whether the channel's content is flagged as mature, affecting its potential audience. Lastly, the Language attribute specifies the primary language used in the channel's broadcasts, which can influence the channel's accessibility to different linguistic demographics.
